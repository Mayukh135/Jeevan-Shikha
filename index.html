Who We Are

Rooted in Communities, Growing Together

Jeevan Shiksha was started in 2017 to work with marginalised communities in Assam and Uttarakhand. In Assam, we partner with Adivasi tea garden communities. In Uttarakhand, we support the Van Gujjar community — many of whom now live in settlements without proper access to healthcare and education.


Our work began with community libraries and has grown to include mental health, livelihoods, menstrual & maternal health, nature education, and access to government services.

Health

MITA — Mental Health Treatment Alliance

Psychiatric services, counselling, medicines, and community field visits — supported by ANT. Awareness street plays performed by university interns break mental health stigma in tea garden communities.

Health

MITA — Mental Health Treatment Alliance

Technical support, medicines, psychiatric services, counselling through a counsellor, and follow-up by a social worker. Field visits assess patient progress. Poster campaigns and street plays raise awareness across the community.

Education

Community Library & Learning Centres

Storytelling sessions, reading programmes, and 3 learning centres in Uttarakhand — supported by BookWorm and the Free Library Network. A pathway to formal school enrolment for Van Gujjar children.

Education

Community Library & Learning Centres

3 active centres at Thanda, Natthu Chhod, and Gujjar Basti with dedicated teachers. Children grouped by learning level. After primary education, older children are enrolled into formal schools. Supported by BookWorm (Goa) and the Free Library Network.
