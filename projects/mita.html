MITA — Mental Health Treatment Alliance

Assam ANT
Overview

About This Programme

MITA (Mental Health Treatment Alliance) is a flagship project supported by ANT. It provides technical support, medicines, psychiatric services, and counselling to individuals in Adivasi tea garden communities of Assam.

A dedicated counsellor provides ongoing support while social workers conduct regular field visits to assess patient progress, educate families about the illness, and ensure treatment continuity. A poster campaign and community street plays help normalise conversations around mental health.

What We Do

Key Activities

MITA Camps

Regular health camps providing psychiatric consultations, medicines, and follow-up care to community members.

Awareness Street Plays

University student interns perform street plays in public spaces to break mental health stigma.

Family Education

Families are educated about illness, treatment plans, and how to support their loved ones.

Poster Campaign

Visual awareness materials displayed across the community to normalise mental health conversations.
